## TL;DR

- The **Geely Geometry C** fills a real gap: 550-600 km real-world range at a price below the [BYD Han](https://blog.ucarsea.com/byd-han-asean-executive-sedan-2026.md) and above the [Atto 3](https://blog.ucarsea.com/byd-atto-3-asean-dealer-sourcing-guide-2026/).
- It is the EV you sell to the buyer who says *"I like the Atto 3 but I drive to the province twice a month."*
- Used 2022-2024 Geometry C clears Phnom Penh landed at $19,500-26,500, Hanoi at $18,500-25,000.
- **Honest caveat**: Geely's ASEAN service network is real but less mature than BYD's. This is the single biggest objection to manage.
- **Customer fit**: regional commuters, small-business owners covering a province, EV-confident buyers who need range insurance.

## Why the Geometry C, and the gap it fills

The Atto 3 is the mass urban EV. The Han is the executive statement. Between them sits a real, underserved buyer: **someone who wants EV economics but regularly drives 300-500 km in a trip.**

That buyer can't trust an Atto 3 (≈325 km real-world — province trip = range anxiety). They won't pay Han money for a sedan they don't need the prestige of. The Geometry C's 550-600 km real-world range is the answer: it makes the inter-province EV trip *boring* instead of *stressful*, at a mid-market price.

Sell the Geometry C to:
- Regional commuters (capital ↔ province twice-weekly drivers).
- Small-business owners whose work covers a province (sales reps, field operators, agri-traders).
- EV-confident second-time buyers trading up from a short-range first EV.
- Buyers who *wanted* an Atto 3 but balked at the range when they thought through their actual driving.

Do not sell the Geometry C to:
- Pure city drivers (overspec — sell the cheaper Atto 3, keep the margin difference as goodwill).
- Buyers in regions with no Geely service presence at all (the support gap is real — be honest).
- Prestige-seekers (the Geometry C reads "smart practical," not "arrived").

## The vehicle

Geely Geometry C — compact crossover EV, in production since 2020 (China), facelifted 2022, exported LHD.

| Spec | 2022-2024 used | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Body | 5-door crossover (4,432 mm) | Atto 3 / Corolla Cross class |
| Battery | 53.1 / 70 kWh ternary (NMC) | Note: NMC, not LFP — different care profile |
| Range (NEDC) | 550 / 620 km | Real-world ~450 / 540 km |
| Motor | 150-204 kW front | Brisker than Atto 3 |
| DC fast charge | ~100 kW (70 kWh pack) | 0-80% ~40 min |
| Drive | FWD | No AWD |

**Chemistry note**: unlike BYD's LFP Blade, the Geometry C uses NMC (ternary) cells — higher energy density (hence the range) but more sensitive to heat and deep-cycling. SoH inspection matters even more here. Source the **70 kWh / 620 km** variant for resale; the 53 kWh is the weaker proposition.

## Battery health — NMC needs closer scrutiny

NMC chemistry degrades differently from LFP — more sensitive to heat soak and fast-charge abuse, which matters in tropical ASEAN deployment.

| SoH | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ≥ 91% | Premium | No concession |
| 85-90% | Good | Standard |
| 79-84% | Fair | -12 to -18%, disclose |
| < 79% | **Reject** | NMC below this degrades faster — don't import |

We hold a stricter floor (79% vs 75% for LFP cars) on Geometry C precisely because NMC degradation accelerates once it starts. Range test under load is mandatory, not optional.

## Service reality — the honest objection

This is the Geometry C's real weakness vs a BYD. Geely's ASEAN authorized network exists (growing through the Proton/Geely Malaysia hub and country distributors) but is **less dense than BYD's** in 2026:

- Capital cities (Phnom Penh, Hanoi, HCMC): Geely service reachable, parts 1-2 week routing.
- Tier-2 cities: thin — often no Geely-specific HV support.
- Wear parts: independents can handle conventional items.
- HV battery / motor: authorized-only, and the network is shallower than BYD's.

**Manage this objection directly with the buyer.** The honest pitch: "The range is the best in this price class. The trade-off is service is thinner than BYD — if you're capital-city based, fine; if you're deep upcountry, consider a [Camry](https://blog.ucarsea.com/toyota-camry-china-lhd-sourcing-asean-2026/) instead." Overselling Geely's support is the fastest way to damage the relationship — see [Parts & Service](https://blog.ucarsea.com/parts-service-japan-vs-china-after-5-years-sea-2026/).
